CSS Pseudo-elements & Pseudo-classes

Pseudo-elements (::) target a sub-part of an element.
Pseudo-classes (:) target an element's state.

Pseudo-elements

::before and ::after

The only way to do great work is to love what you do.

The curly quotes are injected via content in ::before and ::after.

::first-letter

Once upon a time there was a paragraph whose first letter was styled entirely with CSS. No wrapper span needed.

::first-line

This first line is bold and uppercase automatically. Resize the window and watch which words count as the "first line" change dynamically — it's based on rendering, not markup.

::selection

Try selecting this text with your mouse. The highlight color is custom — orange background with dark text instead of the default blue.
